To strengthen cooperation between the parties, and based on the principles of equality, voluntariness, and integrity, Party A and Party B have agreed to jointly invest in the establishment of a joint venture limited liability company (hereinafter referred to as the "Joint Venture") to jointly carry out business activities. Through friendly consultations, the parties have entered into the following agreement:

## II. Capital Contribution and Equity Ratio

1. The amount, form and ratio of capital contribution of Party A and Party B are as follows:

1. Party A's subscribed contribution amount is {{partyAContributionAmount}} in the form of currency, all of which shall be contributed to the registered capital, accounting for {{partyAEquityPercent}}% of the registered capital.

2. Party B's subscribed contribution amount is {{partyBContributionAmount}} in the form of currency, all of which shall be contributed to the registered capital, accounting for {{partyBEquityPercent}}% of the registered capital.

2. Time of Capital Contribution:

Both parties shall, within {{contributionDeadlineDays}} business days after the opening of the Joint Venture Company's bank account, remit the initial capital contribution (not less than {{firstPaymentPercent}}% of their respective subscribed capital) into the account of the Joint Venture Company; the remaining part shall be fully paid up before {{firstPaymentTrigger}}.

Capital Contribution Certificate:
After receiving the full amount of capital contributions, the Joint Venture Company shall engage a local auditor to issue a capital verification report and shall issue Capital Contribution Certificates to both parties.

## III. Business Purpose and Strategic Positioning

The Parties confirm that the core purpose of establishing the Joint Venture Company is to build a regional strategic platform:

Market Development: To conduct market research, brand promotion, and channel development in the {{operatingRegion}} by leveraging the resources of both Parties.

Project Bridging: To provide business opportunity referrals and assist clients in communication and negotiation with local governments or enterprises.

Preliminary Coordination: To serve as a window for the Parties' cooperation in the {{operatingRegion}}, coordinating various matters in the preliminary stages of projects.

For the purposes of this Agreement, the relevant commercial contracts negotiated as from January 1, 2026 shall be signed with Party A prior to the establishment of the joint venture company, provided that the actual benefits of the projects shall belong to the joint venture company being established, and the distribution method shall be as set forth in the joint venture agreement.

## IV. Profit Distribution

1. Distribution Principle: The joint venture shall distribute its distributable profits after taxation for the current year according to the proportion of capital contributions subscribed by both parties.

2. Timing of Distribution: Profit distribution shall be carried out within {{profitDistributionDays}} days after the submission of the annual audit report, the convening of the shareholders' meeting, and the adoption of the dividend resolution.

3. Reserve Fund: Prior to any profit distribution, the Joint Venture Company shall set aside such reserves as required under the {{applicableCompanyLaw}} and the Company’s Articles of Association.

## V. Corporate Governance Structure

Shareholders' Meeting:

The Shareholders' Meeting, composed of all shareholders, shall be the supreme governing body of the Company. The Shareholders' Meeting hereby authorizes the Board of Directors to assume full responsibility for the day-to-day management, operations, and business decision-making of the Company. The Shareholders' Meeting shall retain decision-making authority over the following material matters:

1. Amendments to the articles of association;

2. Increases or decreases in registered capital;

3. Mergers, divisions, dissolution, or change of corporate form; and;

4. Profit distribution plans and loss recovery plans. resolutions on the foregoing matters shall require the unanimous consent of all shareholders;

Other functions and powers, discussion methods, and voting procedures of the Shareholders' Meeting shall be governed by the {{applicableCompanyLaw}} and the Company's Articles of Association.

Board of Directors:

The Company shall establish a Board of Directors, which shall consist of {{totalDirectors}} directors. {{partyADirectors}} director(s) shall be appointed by Party A, and {{partyBDirectors}} director(s) shall be appointed by Party B. The Chairman of the Board (1 seat) shall be nominated by {{chairmanNominatedBy}} and elected by the Board of Directors.

The shareholders hereby authorize the Board of Directors to be responsible for the Company's business operations and to exercise decision-making authority. The specific powers of the Board shall include, but not be limited to:

1. Formulating the company's business plans and investment strategies;

2. Reviewing and approving the company's annual financial budgets and final accounts;

3. Reviewing profit distribution plans and loss recovery plans, and submitting them to the shareholders' meeting for approval;

4. Reviewing proposals to increase or decrease registered capital, and submitting them to the shareholders' meeting for approval; and;

5. Appointing or dismissing the general manager and the chief financial officer, and determining their remuneration. board resolutions shall be made jointly by all directors and shall be valid upon approval by a majority of all directors. matters relating to profit distribution and changes in registered capital shall require the unanimous consent of all directors;

Management: The General Manager (CEO) shall be responsible for the daily operations of the Company, and the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) shall be responsible for financial monitoring and control.

## VI. Confidentiality Clause

1. Both Parties shall keep confidential the contents of this Agreement and any commercial, financial, technical, project-related information, user data, or other documents or information received from the other Party during the performance of this Agreement that are marked confidential (collectively, the "Confidential Information"). Neither Party shall disclose such Confidential Information to any third party outside this Agreement without the prior written consent of the Party disclosing the information (the "Disclosing Party"), except where such disclosure is made:

1. To employees of the receiving Party who have a need to know such Confidential Information solely for the purposes of this Agreement, provided that the receiving Party instructs such employees to comply with the confidentiality and non-disclosure obligations set forth in this Clause.

2. If the documents and information are publicly available, or if their disclosure is required by applicable laws or regulations.
3. To a court or pursuant to any pre-trial discovery procedure or similar procedure, or in connection with any legal proceedings taken in relation to this Agreement.
4. To a regulatory authority, as required by such regulatory authority.
5. In any other circumstances where disclosure is mandatory by law.

2. Neither Party shall disclose to any third party the contents of this Agreement or any business information of the other Party obtained during the performance of this Agreement, unless with the written consent of the other Party.

3. This confidentiality obligation shall survive the expiration, rescission, or termination of this Agreement.

## X. Force Majeure

1. Force majeure referred to in this agreement refers to events such as earthquakes, floods, wars, government actions, and other unforeseeable events whose consequences cannot be reasonably prevented or avoided.

2. If this agreement cannot be performed due to force majeure, the affected party shall immediately notify the other party and provide relevant evidence. Based on the impact of the force majeure, both parties shall negotiate to postpone the performance or terminate the agreement.
