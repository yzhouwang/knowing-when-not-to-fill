## I. Cooperation Content and Objectives

**Article 1: Scope of {{targetCountry}} Project**

The {{targetCountry}} Project refers to any business cooperation, sales, or engineering project conducted between Party A and a qualifying counterparty within {{targetCountry}} with respect to Party A's products and services, including but not limited to servers, HVDC power supply and energy storage, liquid cooling, optical modules and high-speed interconnects, modular AI factories, and data center EPC services.

**Article 2: Rights and Obligations of Party B**

Party A engages Party B to provide consulting services, including market analysis, regulatory and government-relations advice, and the introduction and facilitation of initial contact with potential counterparties in the {{targetCountry}} market, and to assist in arranging initial meetings.

Party B is not authorized to negotiate, sign agreements, or make any binding commitments on behalf of Party A. Party B's role is advisory and introductory in nature.

Party B shall maintain strict confidentiality of all Confidential Information obtained in connection with this cooperation and shall comply with the confidentiality provisions in Section III of this Agreement.

Party B shall promptly disclose any conflict of interest or existing relationship that may affect its independence and objectivity.

**Article 3: Rights and Obligations of Party A**

Party A shall maintain good communication with the counterparties introduced by Party B and shall negotiate in good faith.

If a Qualifying Transaction (as defined in Article 5) is consummated, Party A shall pay Party B the Consulting Fee in accordance with Article 4.

Upon consummation of a transaction, Party A shall notify Party B in writing within ten (10) business days and shall provide necessary transaction information to facilitate fee calculation.

## II. Consulting Fee

**Article 4: Fee Amount**

Upon consummation of a Qualifying Transaction, Party A shall pay Party B a consulting fee equal to {{consultingFeePercent}}% of the total contract value of the Qualifying Transaction, up to a maximum of {{consultingFeeCap}} (the "Consulting Fee").

**Article 5: Qualifying Transaction**

A "Qualifying Transaction" means a signed and binding written contract between the counterparty introduced or advised on by Party B and Party A (or its affiliate) in connection with the {{targetCountry}} Project, under which Party A receives payment or a binding commitment to pay.

**Article 6: Payment Terms**

The Consulting Fee shall be paid on a pro-rata basis in proportion to the project payments actually received by Party A. Within {{paymentTermDays}} days of Party A's receipt of each project payment installment, Party A shall pay Party B the corresponding proportionate share of the Consulting Fee, until the Consulting Fee is paid in full.

Payment shall be made by wire transfer to Party B's designated bank account.

**Article 7: Tax Responsibilities**

Each Party shall be responsible for its own tax obligations arising from this Agreement. Party B acknowledges that it is solely responsible for reporting and paying any income, withholding, or other taxes on the Consulting Fee in its jurisdiction.

**Article 8: No Fee if No Transaction**

For the avoidance of doubt, no Consulting Fee shall be payable if no Qualifying Transaction is consummated, regardless of the efforts expended by Party B.

## III. Confidentiality

**Article 9: Definition and Protection of Confidential Information**

"Confidential Information" means all non-public information received by one Party from the other in connection with this Agreement, including but not limited to business plans, financial data, customer lists, technical specifications, and the terms of this Agreement.

The Receiving Party shall use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing this Agreement and shall not disclose it to any third party without the prior written consent of the disclosing Party, except as required by law.

For information that the Receiving Party must disclose to its representatives on a need-to-know basis, the Receiving Party shall require such representatives to be bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those herein.

The Receiving Party shall employ protective measures no less rigorous than a reasonable degree of care to protect Confidential Information.

The confidentiality obligation shall survive the termination of this Agreement for a period of {{confidentialityTermYears}} years.

**Exceptions:** Information that is publicly available; lawfully obtained from a third party without confidentiality restrictions; independently developed without reference to Confidential Information; or disclosed with prior written consent. When disclosure is required by law, the disclosing Party shall be notified to the extent practicable, and disclosure shall be limited to the minimum extent necessary.
