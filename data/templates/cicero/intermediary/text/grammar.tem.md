## I. Cooperation Content and Objectives

**Article 1: Scope of {{targetCountry}} Project**

The {{targetCountry}} Project refers to any business cooperation, sales, or engineering project conducted between Party A and a qualifying counterparty within {{targetCountry}} with respect to Party A's products and services, including but not limited to servers, HVDC power supply and energy storage, liquid cooling, optical modules and high-speed interconnects, modular AI factories, and data center EPC services.

**Article 2: Rights and Obligations of Party B**

Party A authorizes Party B to introduce and facilitate initial contact with potential counterparties in the {{targetCountry}} market, and to assist in arranging initial meetings.

Party B is not authorized to negotiate, sign agreements, or make any binding commitments on behalf of Party A. Party B's role is limited to introducing counterparties and assisting with initial contact.

Party B shall maintain strict confidentiality of all Confidential Information obtained in connection with this cooperation and shall comply with the confidentiality provisions in Section III of this Agreement.

Party B shall promptly disclose any conflict of interest or existing relationship that may affect its independence and objectivity.

**Article 3: Rights and Obligations of Party A**

Party A shall maintain good communication with the counterparties introduced by Party B and shall negotiate in good faith.

If a Qualifying Transaction (as defined in Article 5) is consummated, Party A shall pay Party B the Finder's Fee in accordance with Article 4.

Upon consummation of a transaction, Party A shall notify Party B in writing within ten (10) business days and shall provide necessary transaction information to facilitate fee calculation.

## II. Finder's Fee

**Article 4: Fee Amount**

Upon consummation of a Qualifying Transaction, Party A shall pay Party B a finder's fee equal to {{finderFeePercent}}% of the total contract value of the Qualifying Transaction, up to a maximum of {{finderFeeCap}} (the "Finder's Fee").

**Article 5: Qualifying Transaction**

A "Qualifying Transaction" means a signed and binding written contract between the counterparty introduced by Party B and Party A (or its affiliate) in connection with the {{targetCountry}} Project, under which Party A receives payment or a binding commitment to pay.

**Article 6: Payment Terms**

The Finder's Fee shall be paid on a pro-rata basis in proportion to the project payments actually received by Party A. Within {{paymentTermDays}} days of Party A's receipt of each project payment installment, Party A shall pay Party B the corresponding proportionate share of the Finder's Fee, until the Finder's Fee is paid in full.

Payment shall be made by wire transfer to Party B's designated bank account.

**Article 7: Tax Responsibilities**

Each Party shall be responsible for its own tax obligations arising from this Agreement. Party B acknowledges that it is solely responsible for reporting and paying any income, withholding, or other taxes on the Finder's Fee in its jurisdiction.

**Article 8: No Fee if No Transaction**

For the avoidance of doubt, no Finder's Fee shall be payable if no Qualifying Transaction is consummated, regardless of the efforts expended by Party B.

## III. Confidentiality

**Article 9: Definition and Protection of Confidential Information**

"Confidential Information" means all non-public information received by one Party from the other in connection with this Agreement, including but not limited to business plans, financial data, customer lists, technical specifications, and the terms of this Agreement.

The Receiving Party shall use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing this Agreement and shall not disclose it to any third party without the prior written consent of the disclosing Party, except as required by law.

For information that the Receiving Party must disclose to its representatives on a need-to-know basis, the Receiving Party shall require such representatives to be bound by confidentiality obligations no less protective than those herein.

The Receiving Party shall employ protective measures no less rigorous than a reasonable degree of care to protect Confidential Information.

The confidentiality obligation shall survive the termination of this Agreement for a period of {{confidentialityTermYears}} years.

**Exceptions:** Information that is publicly available; lawfully obtained from a third party without confidentiality restrictions; independently developed without reference to Confidential Information; or disclosed with prior written consent. When disclosure is required by law, the disclosing Party shall be notified to the extent practicable, and disclosure shall be limited to the minimum extent necessary.

## IV. Anti-Corruption and Compliance

**Article 10: Mutual Anti-Corruption and Compliance Commitment**

Each Party represents and warrants that it shall comply with all applicable anti-bribery and anti-corruption laws, including the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), the UK Bribery Act, and the laws of {{targetCountry}} and Hong Kong. Each Party shall be solely responsible for its own compliance and legality.

Neither Party shall make, promise, offer, or authorize any payment, gift, or benefit of value to any government official, political party, or public entity in connection with the {{targetCountry}} Project. Each Party shall bear sole responsibility for the conduct of its employees and agents.

Each Party represents that it is not a government official or employee of any entity with which the other Party would transact under the {{targetCountry}} Project.

## V. Term and Termination

**Article 11: Agreement Term**

This Agreement shall be effective from the date first written above and shall remain in effect for {{agreementTermMonths}} months. The Agreement may be renewed by mutual written consent.

**Article 12: Termination Notice**

Either Party may terminate this Agreement upon {{terminationNoticeDays}} days' prior written notice.

Either Party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon written notice if the other Party commits a material breach and fails to cure such breach within {{breachCurePeriodDays}} days of receiving written notice thereof.

**Article 13: Per-Project Exclusivity and Expiry**

The Parties shall cooperate on a per-project basis. Each cooperative project shall be confirmed in writing by both Parties through a Project Cooperation Confirmation, specifying the project scope, counterparty, and applicable fee terms. Once a Project Cooperation Confirmation is executed, neither Party shall engage any other supplier or consultant for that project, unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing.

If a customer covered by a Project Cooperation Confirmation has no active bidding, sales, or delivery activity for a continuous period of {{exclusivityExpiryMonths}} months, the relevant Project Cooperation Confirmation shall automatically expire and neither Party shall have further exclusivity obligations with respect to that customer.
